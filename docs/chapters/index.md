# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 23 chapters covering 307 concepts across 4 units, following the College Board AP Pre-Calculus curriculum framework.

## Unit 1: Polynomial and Rational Functions

1. [Mathematical Foundations](01-mathematical-foundations/index.md) - Core building blocks including the real number system, coordinate plane, variables, expressions, and notation systems.
2. [Functions and Their Properties](02-functions-and-their-properties/index.md) - Function definition, notation, evaluation, domain, range, graphing, intercepts, behavior, and symmetry.
3. [Rates of Change](03-rates-of-change/index.md) - How quantities change together, average rate of change, slope, secant lines, concavity, and increasing/decreasing rates.
4. [Linear Functions](04-linear-functions/index.md) - Linear function forms (slope-intercept, point-slope, standard), parallel and perpendicular lines, and linear regression.
5. [Quadratic Functions and Complex Numbers](05-quadratic-functions-and-complex-numbers/index.md) - Quadratic forms, vertex, axis of symmetry, completing the square, quadratic formula, discriminant, and complex numbers.
6. [Polynomial Fundamentals](06-polynomial-fundamentals/index.md) - Polynomial definitions, degree, leading coefficient, operations, factoring, division, and key theorems.
7. [Polynomial Zeros and Graphs](07-polynomial-zeros-and-graphs/index.md) - Zeros, multiplicity, end behavior, turning points, extrema, graphing, and the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.
8. [Rational Functions](08-rational-functions/index.md) - Rational expressions, asymptotes, holes, zeros, domain, end behavior, graphing, and inequalities.

## Transition: Transformations and Modeling

9. [Function Transformations](09-function-transformations/index.md) - Parent functions, translations, stretches, compressions, reflections, combined transformations, and writing transformed equations.
10. [Data Modeling and Regression](10-data-modeling-and-regression/index.md) - Model selection, data analysis, regression, validation, residuals, goodness of fit, and competing models.

## Unit 2: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

11. [Composition and Inverse Functions](11-composition-and-inverse-functions/index.md) - Function composition, domain of compositions, decomposition, inverse functions, one-to-one functions, and horizontal line test.
12. [Sequences](12-sequences/index.md) - Arithmetic and geometric sequences, common difference/ratio, recursive and explicit formulas, convergence and divergence.
13. [Exponential Functions](13-exponential-functions/index.md) - Exponential growth/decay, natural base e, compound interest, continuous growth, equations, graphs, transformations, and modeling.
14. [Logarithmic Functions](14-logarithmic-functions/index.md) - Logarithm definition, properties, equations, graphs, transformations, modeling, semi-log plots, and data linearization.

## Unit 3: Trigonometric and Polar Functions

15. [Angles and the Unit Circle](15-angles-and-the-unit-circle/index.md) - Angle measures, radian/degree conversion, arc length, sector area, standard position, reference angles, unit circle, and special angles.
16. [Trigonometric Functions](16-trigonometric-functions/index.md) - Sine, cosine, tangent, reciprocal functions, evaluation, exact values, right triangle trigonometry, and SOH-CAH-TOA.
17. [Trigonometric Graphs](17-trigonometric-graphs/index.md) - Sine/cosine/tangent graphs, amplitude, period, midline, frequency, phase shift, sinusoidal functions, and reciprocal function graphs.
18. [Sinusoidal Modeling](18-sinusoidal-modeling/index.md) - Graphing trig transformations, sinusoidal modeling, periodic data analysis, real-world models, and sinusoidal regression.
19. [Trigonometric Identities](19-trigonometric-identities/index.md) - Pythagorean, reciprocal, quotient, cofunction, even-odd identities, sum/difference and double angle formulas, verifying and simplifying.
20. [Inverse Trigonometric Functions and Equations](20-inverse-trig-functions-and-equations/index.md) - Arcsine, arccosine, arctangent, inverse trig domains/ranges/graphs, solving trig equations, general solutions, and inequalities.

## Unit 4: Functions Involving Parameters, Vectors, and Matrices

21. [Polar and Parametric Functions](21-polar-and-parametric-functions/index.md) - Polar coordinates, conversions, polar graphs, parametric equations, motion, and projectile models.
22. [Vectors](22-vectors/index.md) - Vector definition, notation, magnitude, direction, component form, unit vectors, operations, dot product, and applications.
23. [Matrices and Exam Preparation](23-matrices-and-exam-preparation/index.md) - Matrix operations, determinant, inverse, linear transformations, applications, and AP exam strategies.

## How to Use This Textbook

Chapters are sequenced to respect concept dependencies — each chapter builds on concepts from earlier chapters. Students should progress through the chapters in order, though Unit 4 content is not tested on the AP exam and may be studied independently. Each chapter includes a list of concepts covered and links to prerequisite chapters.
